Sketching ideas for VR locomotion

February 5, 2022

For this assignment, I imagined three ways for a user to move in a VR scene. I chose three virtual locomotions (where the user doesn’t actually walk), with different systems, all aiming to reduce simulator sickness, which can be a very unpleasant side effect of VR locomotion.

Clone Dash#

In this idea, the user controls a clone of himself using the joystick. It can move around like a character in classical video games. Then, by pressing a button, the user triggers a “dash” (inspired by the “Blink”, in the game Dishonored), and rushes toward the clone. The fact that the rush is quite fast, as well as a blur effect on the sides of the screen, reduces simulator sickness.

Slide the World#

Often, simulator sickness occurs in VR because of an inconsistency between the static body of the user, and their impression of moving in the virtual world. But what if the world was moving, and not the user ?

In this idea, the user uses a the controller as anchor points to move the world around him the way he wants to, by “pinching” the environment, moving it around (pulling, pushing and rotating) with his hands, and then releasing.

Miniature World#

In this idea, the user manipulates with his hands a small puppet representing himself in the world. By turning his left hand up, the user makes a miniature version of his environment appear. By then placing the puppet at the desired spot on the miniature, they trigger a teleportation to the new spot.
