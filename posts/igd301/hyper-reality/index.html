Hyper Reality, and ideas of harmful VR

February 4, 2022

Hyper Reality#

Ideas of harmful MR#

Hyper Reality is a very good example of a distopian world, where MR is used to the worst of its potential. Here are two ways Mixed Reality could be used to intentionally cause harm (physical or not) to its users.

Causing physical harm by misleading the user#

By putting interesting objects at specific spots in the virtual world, one can lure the user, lead them to a particular direction. But if the user actually walks there, they might face danger in the real world, to which they are not paying attention (in the case of VR, they cannot even see it !).

In this example, the user is playing a VR game where he has to collect coins. But the friendly virtual environment is very different from the real world, and the user does not realize he’s putting himself in danger by crossing a road. If the creator is deliberately luring the user to this dangerous position, this is an abusive situation.

One could think that failsafes such as the Oculus Guardian, or the simple fact that in Augmented Reality, not all the environment is occluded, this kind of danger could be avoided. And yet, when faced with thrilling games, sometimes with addictive components, people are inclined to have a “tunnel vision” effect, or to consciously ignore the failsafes.

Stealing personal data and using it for criminal intent#

In this idea, a malicious individual gathers and uses a user’s personal data to create a “Deep Fake” copy of them, and steal money from their close ones.

This is of course abusive, as it falls into the definition of identity theft. There is already some legal protection against this kind af acts. But it is hard to control how huge companies use the data they collect. And even if the companies themselves don’t do anything wrong, cyber-attacks can allow hackers to steal data from them and use them for their own purposes.
